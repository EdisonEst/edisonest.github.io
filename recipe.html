Crispy Fried Chicken

History

With all the prep work, fried chicken was by definition special-occasionfood, and for most African Americans, special occasions were communal meals connected to their church. Today, fried chicken is less about church and more about family.

The Sacred Roots of Fried Chicken

Recipe

This is the only crispy fried chicken recipe you'll ever need. Why? It combines all the tried-and-true tricks to ensure the juiciest, most flavorful, crispiest chicken every time.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps to Make

  1. Take your cut up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer.
  2. Put the flour in a large plastic bag (let the amount of chicken you are cooking dictate the amount of flour you use).
    Season the flour with paprika, salt and pepper to taste (paprika helps to brown the chicken).
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk then, a few at a time, put them in the bag with the flour, seal the bag
    and shake to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper.
  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until VERY hot.
  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in HOT oil on both sides.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; let cook for 30 minutes (the chicken will be cooked through
    but not crispy). Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.
  8. Drain the fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have
    to fry in a few shifts. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while preparing the rest.
